Explore Naga Flavors

Naga Flavors

Discover the rich flavors of Bicol—where tradition, spice, and creativity come together.

1. Kinalas

This unique noodle soup, born in Naga, features a savory, thick brown gravy made from boiled beef or pork head meat scraped (kinalas) from the bone. It is considered Naga's signature noodle dish.

2. Toasted Siopao

A distinct Naga variation of the popular siopao where the bun is baked until golden brown. A must-try snack.

3. Silli Ice Cream

This dessert blends creamy ice cream with chili peppers. Reflects Bicolanos' love for spicy food in a sweet, cold treat.

4. Bicol Express

A rich, fiery stew made from sliced pork and shrimp paste, slow-cooked in coconut milk and chili peppers.

5. Pinangat (or Laing)

Shredded taro leaves with a filling, wrapped in taro leaves, and stewed in creamy coconut milk.

6. Kinunot

Flaked stingray or shark meat cooked with malunggay leaves in rich, spicy coconut cream sauce.

7. Pili Nut

Native nuts transformed into caramelized or brittle candies. Popular local souvenir (pasalubong).

8. Pancit Bato

Sun-dried noodles from Bato municipality, sautéed for a local favorite snack.

9. Ginataang Tilapia

Fish cooked in seasoned coconut milk highlighting local freshness and gata usage.

10. Laing Pizza

Modern dish using taro-leaf-and-coconut-milk stew as topping. Creative Bicolano fusion.
